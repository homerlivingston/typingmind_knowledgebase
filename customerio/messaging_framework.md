# Messaging Framework - Spot Pet Insurance

**Purpose:** Guide consistent, persona-aligned messaging across all customer.io campaigns
**Last Updated:** November 17, 2025

---

## Brand Voice & Tone

### Core Brand Attributes
- **Helpful:** Pet parents' trusted partner in pet health
- **Straightforward:** Clear, jargon-free communication
- **Warm:** Caring and empathetic about pets
- **Professional:** Reliable insurance expertise

### Tone by Journey Stage

| Journey Stage | Tone | Why |
|--------------|------|-----|
| Hunt (Pre-Purchase) | Educational, reassuring | Build trust, reduce uncertainty |
| Golden (Active Policy) | Welcoming, supportive | Reinforce decision, drive engagement |
| Lapsed (Payment Failed) | Urgent but helpful | Motivate action without alienating |
| Canceled (Win-back) | Understanding, value-focused | Re-engage without pressure |

## Customer Personas

Spot targets three primary personas with distinct psychographic profiles. While campaigns don't currently segment explicitly by persona, messaging should resonate with these core motivations.

### 1. Experimental Fun Seeker

**Core Message:** *"Life's an adventure. Pet insurance shouldn't be."*

**Characteristics:**
- High AOV potential
- Wellness plan adopters
- Value premium service experiences
- Seek unique, engaging experiences

**Messaging Approach:**
- **Bold, vibrant language**
- **Adventure and confidence themes**
- **Premium brand positioning**
- **Unique product experiences highlighted**
- **Upbeat, energetic tone**

**Example Themes:**
- "Adventure-proof your pet's health"
- "Explore life worry-free with [Pet Name]"
- "Premium coverage for premium adventures"

---

- **Minimal details, maximum clarity**

- **Flexible, uncomplicated positioning**
- **Light, accessible tone**

**Example Themes:**

## Messaging by Journey Stage

### Hunt Journey (Pre-Purchase)

**Primary Objective:** Education and conversion

**Key Message Pillars:**
1. **Coverage Value**
   - What's covered vs. not covered
   - Pre-existing condition clarity
   - Hereditary/congenital coverage
   - Wellness plan benefits

2. **Financial Clarity**
   - Transparent pricing
   - Reimbursement examples
   - Real vet bill scenarios
   - Plan comparison guidance

3. **Trust Building**
   - Sample policy documents
   - Customer testimonials
   - Claims process transparency
   - No-surprise positioning

4. **Urgency (Gentle)**
   - Quote reminders
   - Coverage waiting periods
   - Pet age considerations
   - Limited-time offers (if applicable)

**Segment-Specific Variations:**

**Sam's Club:**
- Emphasize 15% exclusive discount
- "Sam's Club member benefit"
- Partnership value messaging
- Custom plan options

**API Partners (Comparison Shoppers):**
- Competitive differentiation
- Superior coverage highlights
- Value proposition clarity
- Why Spot stands out

**MSN/Microsoft:**
- [Specific messaging rationale TBD]
- Currently uses distinct email templates
- SMS uses standard Direct messaging

**Direct (Organic):**
- Standard education-focused messaging
- Broad appeal across personas
- Comprehensive coverage explanations

---

### Golden Journey (Post-Purchase)

**Primary Objective:** Onboarding, engagement, retention

**Key Message Pillars:**
1. **Welcome & Validation**
   - Congratulations on smart decision
   - Coverage now active
   - What to expect next

2. **Claims Education**
   - How to submit a claim
   - Required documentation
   - Reimbursement timeline
   - Mobile app for easy claims

3. **Coverage Reminders**
   - What's covered
   - Wellness benefits
   - Policy documents
   - Member portal access

4. **Engagement & Value**
   - Mobile app features
   - Spot Perks rewards
   - Pet health tips
   - Customer service resources

---

### Lapsed Journey (Payment Recovery)

**Primary Objective:** Payment recovery with escalating urgency

**Message Progression:**

**Day 0-2 (Informative):**
- "Payment issue detected"
- "Update your payment method"
- Helpful, solution-oriented tone

**Day 6-14 (Direct):**
- "Account past due"
- "Coverage at risk"
- Clear consequences, clear solution

**Day 24+ (Urgent):**
- "Final notice"
- "Coverage will be canceled"
- Critical urgency, last chance

**Consistent Elements:**
- Easy payment update links
- Customer service contact
- Empathy for payment issues
- No judgment, only solutions

---

### Canceled Journey (Win-Back)

**Primary Objective:** Re-engagement and re-enrollment

**Key Message Pillars:**
1. **Understanding**
   - Acknowledge cancellation
   - No hard feelings
   - Door remains open

2. **Value Reminders**
   - What they're missing
   - Coverage benefits recap
   - Customer testimonials

3. **Re-enrollment Path**
   - Easy to come back
   - Previous coverage details
   - New quote or reactivation

## Multi-Channel Strategy

### Email vs. SMS

**Email Best For:**
- Detailed education
- Policy documents
- Long-form content
- Visual elements (vet bills, infographics)
- Multi-step explanations

**SMS Best For:**
- Urgent notifications
- Quick reminders
- High-priority CTAs
- Time-sensitive messages
- Payment alerts

### Channel Coordination

**Transactional Messages:**
- Email: Comprehensive details
- SMS: Immediate alert with key info

**Hunt Campaigns:**
- Email: Educational content, coverage details
- SMS: Reminders, quote expiration, special offers

**Golden Journey:**
- Email: Welcome, claims guides, engagement content
- SMS: Service value props, app download prompts

**Lapsed Journey:**
- Email: Payment update instructions, coverage loss details
- SMS: Urgent payment alerts, deadline reminders

## Content Guidelines

### Subject Lines (Email)

**Best Practices:**
- 40-60 characters optimal
- Front-load value or urgency
- Personalize with pet name when possible
- Emoji use: Sparingly and brand-appropriate
- A/B test variations

**Examples by Journey:**
- **Hunt:** "Your [Pet Name] quote is ready" / "Pet insurance made simple"
- **Golden:** "Welcome to Spot, [Pet Parent]!" / "How to file your first claim"
- **Lapsed:** "Payment issue with [Pet Name]'s policy" / "Final notice: Update payment"

### SMS Copy

**Best Practices:**
- 160 characters or less
- Clear, direct CTA
- Include link for easy action
- Avoid jargon
- Respectful frequency

**Examples:**
- **Hunt:** "Hi [Name], your pet insurance quote is ready. View now: [link]"
- **Lapsed:** "Your payment failed. Update now to keep [Pet Name] covered: [link]"

### Personalization

**Available Tokens:**
- Pet name(s)
- Pet parent name
- Quote details
- Policy information
- Coverage start date
- Payment amount
- Custom attributes

**Use Cases:**
- Subject lines (pet name)
- Email body (pet parent name)
- Coverage details (plan specifics)
- Payment reminders (amount due)

## A/B Testing Framework

### Elements to Test

**Subject Lines:**
- Question vs. statement
- Urgency vs. value
- Personalization vs. generic
- Length variations

**Message Content:**
- Persona-aligned variations
- CTA placement and wording
- Image vs. text-heavy
- Short vs. long form

**Send Timing:**
- Time of day
- Day of week
- Delay between messages
- Conditional waits (open-based)

**Segment Variations:**
- Sam's vs. Direct messaging
- API vs. Direct positioning
- Species-specific (dog vs. cat)

### Testing Process
1. **Hypothesis:** Clear prediction of better performer
2. **Random Cohort Split:** 50/50 or other split
3. **Success Metric:** Opens, clicks, conversions
4. **Statistical Significance:** Sufficient sample size
5. **Winner Implementation:** Roll out to 100%
6. **Document Learnings:** Update messaging guidelines

## Message Naming Conventions

### Campaign Naming
Format: `[CHANNEL]-[STAGE]-[NUMBER]-[THEME]-[AUDIENCE]-[TYPE]-[VERSION]`

Examples:
- `SAMS-EML-0-KNOWSAMSDISCOUNT-DIR-CUR-1`
- `PHNT-EML-6-WellnessPlans-PLN-MVP-7`
- `LAPS-SMS-2-PAYMENTFAILED-ALL-URG-3`

**Abbreviations:**
- **CHANNEL:** EML (email), SMS, PUSH
- **STAGE:** HUNT, GOLD (golden), LAPS (lapsed), CANC (canceled)
- **AUDIENCE:** DIR (direct), API, SAMS, MSN, ALL
- **TYPE:** CUR (current offer), EDU (educational), URG (urgent), REM (reminder), etc.
